Your friend, Zoo Ossped, was driving through Pendleton, Oregon and bought a pair of yellow hipster jeans (pendlepants) from a thrift store. They were such a big hit with his wife and co-workers he bought 3 more (turquoise, red, and peanut-butter).

Later that month, Zoo took a trip to Seattle with his pendlepants and converted 10-speed to ride in a fixie parade. Dissilusioned, Zoo realized that he didn't measure up to the real hipsters in Seattle with just his pendlepants. These mustached hipsters paired their hipster jeans with commensurate hipster tank tops.

Of course, Zoo bought several before the sun could set on the space needle. While he was at it, he bought a few hipster frames to claim his spot in hipster history.

Given a wardrobe, print the unique outfits Zoo Ossped can put together when he gets home.

Input

Each line of the input will be a space separated list of items for one position on the body.

Output

Each line of output should represent one outfit, combining one item of each type (space separated). Outfits and outfit items should preserve the input orderings.

Example

STDIN:

pendlepants turqoise
prison-stripe mesh
waldo horn-rim

STDOUT:

pendlepants prison-stripe waldo
pendlepants prison-stripe horn-rim
pendlepants mesh waldo
pendlepants mesh horn-rim
turqoise prison-stripe waldo
turqoise prison-stripe horn-rim
turqoise mesh waldo
turqoise mesh horn-rim
